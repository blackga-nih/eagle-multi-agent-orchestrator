• The Compliance Strategist Agent Risk Triggers:
	1. Sole source determination without clear FAR 6.302 authority match
	2. Small business set-aside bypass in competitive acquisition >$250K
	3. Total small business program exclusion (no set-aside, no subcontracting plan)
	4. Section 508 waiver or exception request required
	5. Foreign contractor involvement requiring security review
	6. Procurement method deviation from agency standard practices
	7. Multiple regulatory waivers or exceptions in single acquisition
	8. Executive Order compliance issues (Buy American, domestic preferences)
	9. Consolidation of previously separate contracts >$2M threshold
	10. Use of emergency acquisition authorities outside genuine emergency
	11. Contract terms conflicting with mandatory FAR clauses
	12. Acquisition crossing multiple fiscal years without proper authority
	
• The Legal Counselor Agent Risk Triggers:
	1. Sole source >$10M without FAR 6.302(c)(1) "only one source" justification OR incumbent advantage with >3 years continuous performance + limited competition OR technical requirements cite specific brand names/proprietary solutions
	2. Intellectual property rights requiring government purpose licenses
	3. Data rights negotiations involving contractor proprietary information
	4. Contract disputes history with incumbent or proposed contractors
	5. Organizational conflict of interest (OCI) potential requiring mitigation
	6. International agreements or treaties affecting acquisition approach
	7. Environmental compliance requirements with legal liability exposure
	8. Labor law issues (Service Contract Act, Davis-Bacon) applicability questions
	9. Export control or technology transfer restrictions (ITAR, EAR)
	10. Subcontractor flow-down requirements with compliance verification challenges
	11. Termination for cause risk assessment indicating contractor performance concerns
	12. Contract modification authority limits requiring senior approval
	13. Government-furnished property/equipment with liability allocation issues
	
• The Market Intelligence & Small Business Advocate Agent Risk Triggers:
	1. Zero commercial sources identified for government-unique requirement
	2. Market research indicates only 1-2 potential sources available
	3. Market research identifies <3 small businesses with relevant past performance OR no small businesses with >$5M similar contract awards in past 3 years
	4. Industry consolidation creating limited competition concerns
	5. Set-aside determination bypassing small business opportunities without justification
	6. RFI recommendation to determine market capabilities
	7. Sources sought notice required due to uncertain market response
	8. Market research results contradicting assumed acquisition strategy
	9. Technology <3 years since commercial introduction OR >30% change in vendor market share within past 18 months OR industry standards updated within past 12 months
	10. Total small business program exclusion (no set-aside, no subcontracting plan)
	11. Potential bundling of dissimilar requirements affecting small business participation
	12. Industry feedback suggesting competition restrictions too narrow
	13. Small business subcontracting plan waiver requests
	14. Market pricing volatility indicating need for economic price adjustments
	15. New market entrants disrupting established vendor relationships
	16. Vendor capacity constraints potentially affecting delivery schedules
	17. HUBZone, SDVOSB, or WOSB set-aside opportunities not properly evaluated
	18. Market research age exceeding recommended update periods for dynamic markets
	
• The CO-COR Liaison & Technical Translator Agent Risk Triggers:
		1. 5 technical evaluation factors/subfactors OR evaluation requires security clearances not held by 80% of available evaluators OR technical demonstration/live testing required
	2. COR delegation authority questions for proposed contract administration tasks
	3. Performance measurement standards requiring government surveillance capabilities
	4. Quality Assurance Surveillance Plan (QASP) development needs specialist input
	5. Contractor performance history indicating intensive oversight requirements
	6. Multiple COR appointment needs across different technical disciplines
	7. Government-furnished information/property coordination across multiple organizations
	8. Deliverable acceptance criteria requiring specialized government expertise
	9. Contract administration workload exceeding typical COR capacity
	10. Technical specification disputes requiring CO-level arbitration
	11. Performance-based contracting metrics needing government validation capabilities
	12. Contractor access requirements (facilities, systems, personnel) requiring security coordination
	
• The Financial Advisor Agent Risk Triggers:
	1. IGCE analysis shows <15% cost difference between FFP vs T&M approaches OR requirements include both defined deliverables and level-of-effort tasks OR performance period >12 months with uncertain scope evolution
	2. Cost analysis indicating >25% variance from historical pricing
	3. IGCE total exceeding approved budget by >10%
	4. Contract value approaching CO delegation authority limits
	5. Multi-year funding strategy requiring complex appropriation management
	6. Economic price adjustment clauses needed due to market volatility
	7. Fee/profit margins outside normal ranges for contract type
	8. Payment terms deviating from standard government practices
	9. Cost-reimbursement contract without adequate cost accounting system
	10. Incentive contracting opportunities with significant performance risk
	11. Option pricing uncertainty due to market volatility
	12. Life-cycle cost analysis showing unfavorable total ownership costs
	
• The Public Interest Guardian Agent Risk Triggers:
	1. Overall acquisition risk score exceeding agency threshold levels
	2. Schedule risk indicating >50% probability of delivery delays
	3. Contractor CPARS shows >1 "marginal" rating in past 3 years OR contractor has >2 cure notices in similar contracts within 24 months OR key personnel turnover >40% on similar contracts
	4. Technical risk involving unproven or developmental technologies
	5. Financial risk from contractor business stability concerns
	6. Security risk requiring enhanced background investigation procedures
	7. Reputational risk to agency from high-visibility acquisition failure
	8. Regulatory compliance risk in evolving policy environment
	9. Supply chain risk from sole-source components or foreign dependencies
	10. Integration risk when multiple contractors must work together
	11. Political risk from Congressional or stakeholder attention potential
	12. Operational risk if acquisition failure disrupts critical agency missions
