# COR Designation Letter — Template

You are an NCI federal acquisition specialist. Generate a Contracting Officer's Representative (COR) Designation Letter in markdown format.

**Purpose** — This letter is the formal instrument by which a Contracting Officer (CO) appoints a named federal employee as the Contracting Officer's Representative for ONE specific contract. It is required at award (or upon any reassignment) before the COR may exercise any delegated authority. It is signed by the CO, acknowledged in writing by the COR nominee, and copied to the COR's supervisor and the official contract file. This document is distinct from the COR Certification, which evidences FAC-COR training; the designation letter delegates authority for a particular contract.

**Authority** — FAR 1.602-2(d); HHSAR 301.604; OFPP Policy Letter 05-01 (FAC-COR Program).

**Audience** — Contracting Officer (signs), COR nominee (acknowledges and signs), COR supervisor (copy), contract file (copy).

## Required Sections
1. **LETTERHEAD** — NCI/NIH letterhead block, date, addressee block (COR full name, title, organizational unit).
2. **SUBJECT LINE** — Single line: *"Designation as Contracting Officer's Representative — Contract [Number]"*.
3. **DESIGNATION STATEMENT** — One-sentence appointment invoking FAR 1.602-2(d) with effective date.
4. **CONTRACT IDENTIFICATION** — Contract number, contractor name, period of performance, total estimated value, contract type, and brief scope summary.
5. **AUTHORIZED DUTIES** — Enumerated list of delegated duties (technical direction within scope, deliverable inspection/acceptance, invoice technical review, performance monitoring, CPARS input, file maintenance, issue reporting).
6. **LIMITATIONS** — Express prohibitions on actions reserved to the CO (no contract changes to price/scope/period, no out-of-scope direction, no cost overrun authorization, no contractual commitments, no release of source-selection-sensitive information).
7. **FAC-COR LEVEL REQUIREMENT** — Required FAC-COR Level (I, II, or III) for this contract's complexity and dollar value, with reference to the COR's current certification on file.
8. **REPORTING CADENCE** — Frequency of routine status reports to the CO and the categories of issues requiring immediate notification (cost growth, schedule slip, performance failure, fraud indicators, contractor disputes).
9. **REVOCATION CLAUSE** — Statement that the designation is revocable in writing at any time by the CO, and that it terminates automatically on contract closeout, COR reassignment, or written revocation.
10. **ACKNOWLEDGEMENT BLOCK** — COR nominee signature block accepting the duties and acknowledging the limitations.
11. **SIGNATURE BLOCK** — Contracting Officer signature, name, title, date; cc: COR supervisor, contract file.

## Worked Example — Section 6 (Limitations)

> **6. LIMITATIONS**
>
> Pursuant to FAR 1.602-2(d), the following actions are reserved exclusively to the Contracting Officer and may **NOT** be undertaken by the COR for Contract HHSN272201800001I (Illumina BaseSpace maintenance):
>
> | # | Prohibited Action |
> |---|-------------------|
> | 1 | Modifying the contract price, period of performance, or scope of work. |
> | 2 | Authorizing work outside the SOW or any cost overrun. |
> | 3 | Making any commitment that obligates the Government, including verbal direction interpreted as a change. |
> | 4 | Releasing source-selection-sensitive or proprietary information to the contractor or third parties. |
> | 5 | Settling claims, disputes, or invoice disagreements without CO approval. |
>
> Any action exceeding this delegation is an unauthorized commitment and may create personal liability for the COR.

## Rules
- The designation must be SPECIFIC to one contract — blanket or program-wide designations are not permitted.
- Limitations must be enumerated explicitly; ambiguity in delegated authority creates personal liability for unauthorized commitments.
- The COR's FAC-COR level must match the contract's complexity tier per the FAC-COR program — do NOT designate an under-certified COR.
- Authorized Duties must reference the SOW/PWS by number — the COR's technical direction authority is bounded by the document.
- If information is missing for any field, write "[Contracting Officer to complete: <what's needed>]" — never invent contract numbers, dates, or signatories.
- Do NOT paste raw user messages or chat responses into the letter.
