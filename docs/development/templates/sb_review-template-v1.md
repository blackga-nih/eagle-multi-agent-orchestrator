# HHS-653 Small Business Review — Template

**Purpose**: The HHS-653 Small Business Review documents the contracting officer's analysis of small business participation opportunities for a proposed acquisition. It is required for any procurement above the simplified acquisition threshold (and recommended below) and determines (1) the appropriate set-aside path, (2) NAICS code suitability and the corresponding SBA size standard, and (3) whether the FAR 19.502 "Rule of Two" supports a small business set-aside. The completed form drives the agency's set-aside decision before solicitation issuance.

**Authority**: FAR Part 19 (Small Business Programs), HHSAR Part 319 (HHS supplement), FAR 19.502 (Rule of Two), SBA size standards (13 CFR 121).

**Audience**: Small Business Specialist (concurrence), Contracting Officer (determination), Source Selection Authority (review).

You are an NCI federal acquisition specialist. Generate an HHS-653 Small Business Review in markdown format per HHSAR Part 319 and FAR Part 19.

## Required Sections
1. FORM HEADER — Requirement title, estimated value, NAICS code, SBA size standard, contracting activity, requesting office, date
2. REQUIREMENT SUMMARY — 2-3 sentences describing what is being acquired and the period of performance
3. SMALL BUSINESS PROGRAM ASSESSMENT — Yes/No determination grid covering the 5 set-aside paths (8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB/EDWOSB, SB Set-Aside under FAR 19.502) with rationale and market-research citation per row
4. MARKET RESEARCH BASIS — Cite the specific market research supporting each Yes/No (sources sought results, SAM.gov searches, prior-procurement history, Dynamic Small Business Search hits)
5. NAICS / PSC SIZE STANDARD ANALYSIS — Confirm the selected NAICS, the corresponding 13 CFR 121.201 size standard (receipts or employees), and whether the requirement is properly classified
6. RULE OF TWO DETERMINATION — Per FAR 19.502-2, document whether there is a reasonable expectation of receiving offers from at least two responsible small businesses at fair market prices
7. RECOMMENDATION — Recommended set-aside path (or unrestricted with substantive rationale) and the supporting justification
8. SMALL BUSINESS SPECIALIST CONCURRENCE — Name, signature line, date, concurrence/non-concurrence note
9. CONTRACTING OFFICER DETERMINATION — Name, signature line, date, final determination statement

## Rules
- The "Rule of Two" (FAR 19.502-2(b)) applies above the simplified acquisition threshold — document the determination explicitly with named sources, not boilerplate
- Recommending against a set-aside requires citation to specific market-research evidence (sources sought results, SAM.gov queries, DSBS searches) — generic statements like "no small businesses identified" are insufficient
- Cite the exact 13 CFR 121.201 size standard for the selected NAICS (receipts threshold or employee count) — do not omit
- Tier-one socioeconomic set-asides (8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB, WOSB) must be considered BEFORE a general small business set-aside per FAR 19.203
