## Key Decisions

### 1. Checklist Must Be Staged (Pizza Tracker Model)

Do not present the full compliance checklist at once. Break it into three stages that gate on completion of the prior stage:

| Stage | Trigger | Content |
|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Basic intake complete | Pathway determination items |
| 2 | Pathway confirmed | Required documents for that path |
| 3 | Docs submitted / milestones hit | Special/late-emerging documents |

Users need a sense of accomplishment and forward progression. Showing 1,000 items at once defeats the purpose.

### 2. Reduce Output Verbosity

The full compliance report with all FAR clauses is overkill for a working session. Each response should surface only:

- What was just accomplished (confirmation feedback loop)
- What needs to happen right now (next action)

Items in a "maybe later" category and noise entries (e.g., FAR 8.8 printing services) must be suppressed.

### 3. Pre-Filtering with Quick Form Cards

Bring back clickable form cards in chat for the 5–13 basic intake questions. Clicking is faster than typing for structured inputs. The AI analyzes answers to determine the starting acquisition pathway. Ryan confirmed this is the right approach and aligns with how experienced users want to move quickly while new users get the guided experience.

### 4. Defer Document Generation

Do not auto-generate documents until the user explicitly requests them or the acquisition path is confirmed. Collect atomic-level information first, then assemble documents from that structured data.

### 5. Vehicle/Mechanism Recommendations Must Be Ranked

Show the top recommendation with reasoning, plus alternative options with pros/cons. Do not default to a single path (e.g., Part 15 open market) when GSA/NITAAC may be the better choice. Legacy Eagle ranked options well — new EAGLE must replicate this behavior.

### 6. Micro Purchase Flow Correction

Legacy Eagle handles micro purchases correctly. New EAGLE must:

- Not generate a SOW for micro purchases
- Use a purchase card transaction form (statement of need)
- Include post-purchase steps (delivery, accountability)

### 7. Policy Update Workflow (Manual Phase)

Policy managers receive read access plus the ability to comment or request changes. The dev team reviews, approves, regenerates the compliance matrix, and reports back to both parties. This is manual for the current phase; automation is planned.

## Discussion Notes

**On asking baseline questions upfront**: Ryan and Greg aligned that every session should open with four questions: (1) new vs. existing acquisition, (2) do you have baseline documents, (3) role/position, (4) budget range. These four answers unlock the correct pathway without back-and-forth.

**On timeline dates**: Dates are only meaningful when milestones exist and are set by the CO at AP signing. The system should not fabricate or backfill dates.

**On QASP**: QASP is not universally required. The system should check whether performance-based requirements are present before flagging it.

**On document templates**: PWS template is missing from the knowledge base. Ryan will source or create it. A sample AP (redacted or dummy) is also needed for testing.

**On legacy Eagle value**: Ryan noted the scoring matrix for vehicle selection was a standout capability. The new EAGLE must preserve and improve that logic.
